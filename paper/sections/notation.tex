\section{Notation}

\begin{definition}[Collatz map]
Define $T:\mathbb{N}\to\mathbb{N}$ by
\[
T(n) =
\begin{cases}
  n/2, & \text{if $n$ is even},\\
  3n+1, & \text{if $n$ is odd}.
\end{cases}
\]
The \emph{Collatz trajectory} of $n$ is the sequence $n, T(n), T^{2}(n), \dots$.
\end{definition}

\begin{conjecture}[Collatz]
For every $n\in\mathbb{N}$, there exists $k\ge 0$ such that $T^{k}(n)=1$.
\end{conjecture}

We write:
- $\sigma(n)$ for the (total) stopping time: the smallest $k$ such that $T^{k}(n)=1$, when it exists.
- $\pi(n)$ for the peak value along the trajectory up to time $\sigma(n)$.

\paragraph{Implementation note.}
Our canonical implementation lives in `src/collatz/` and scripts in `scripts/` generate paper figures.
